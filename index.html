Research

2018.05 - 2018.08: Paramekanti Group, University of Toronto

Worked on a study of magnetoelectric effects induced by spontaneous symmetry breaking in strongly correlated materials (arXiv:1904.04280).

  • Participated in the development of the research question.
  • Developed code to calculate the kinetic magnetoelectric response of a crystal.
  • Applied the code to a specific model exhibiting the effect in question.
  • Interpreted the features of numerical results using analytical calculations and symmetry considerations.

2017.09 - 2019.04: Steinberg Lab, University of Toronto

Worked in the Bose-Einstein Condensate group to build and test a 405nm interference-filter based external cavity diode laser (ECDL).

  • Showed that the ECDL can stably maintain single-mode operation.
  • Characterized ECDL frequency and power dependence on temperature, external cavity length, and interference filter angle.
  • Modified the design to improve the tolerance on the machined components.

2017.05 - 2017.08: Netterfield Lab, University of Toronto

Involved in preparing the baloon-borne telescope SuperBIT for a test flight at the Columbia Scientific Baloon Facility in Palestine, Texas. Also took part in the data analysis of teh SPIDER collaboration.
  • Helped with assembling SuperBIT and testing the optics at the launch facility
  • Built mock solar panels to test for effects of added weight on SuperBIT's stability
  • Searched for an elusive noise pattern that appeared in the SPIDER data by curve-fitting and correlating the data with known examples of the noise
  • Successfully identified almost 200 instances of the noie that were previously missed
